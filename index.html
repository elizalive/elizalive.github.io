I pitched, booked, recorded, co-wrote and produced this interview with writer and producer David Simon about his HBO series "Show Me a Hero." Simon and guest host Pamela Kirkland discuss how housing policy created racial and economic segregation in American cities.

When DecodeDC writer Dick Meyer penned a thank-you note to the President Obama in his weekly column, it struck quite a chord with our audience. The piece had more than a million unique views across 24 sites, and thousands of readers shared it on social media. I recorded Dick reading the piece, set the narration to images and some music, and produced this short video to capitalize on the piece's popularity.

I created this interactive to accompany a Scripps investigation of ATV regulations. I used Project Thunderdome's excellent profiler app as a starting point and customized as necessary.

This animation started with an interview I did with researcher Stephanie Coontz about the complicated story of marriage in human history. I wrote and produced this explainer, with illustration help from artist Annie Brown. DecodeDC used the animation during a live event in partnership with WCPO Cincinnati around the changing face of marriage.

When Rand Paul threw his hat in the Presidential ring, writer Abby Johnston and I teamed up to create this unconventional comparison. We collaborated on the writing and I created the illustration for DecodeDC.

I pitched, co-reported and produced this story with Robert Smith, of NPR's Planet Money Team. Together, we explore why it's so hard to figure out whether the money the U.S. spends on grants and loans to college students is actually paying off.

When President Obama announced his plan to make community college free, I pitched, reported, voiced and produced this episode to give listeners a sense of what free community college looked like in a place that had tried it before: Tennessee. Turns out, Obama's plan may have been missing a key ingredient.

For election night 2014, I designed and built this visualization to track the results of the most critical U.S. Senate races in real time. I used JSON to connect the visualization to a Google Spreadsheet that I updated on election night, which was published on the DecodeDC election night live blog.

I wrote and produced this explainer video to outline key differences between two pieces of legislation inspired by Scripps' Peabody-winning story about military sex offenders. Voiced by Scripps National Correspondent Mark Greenblatt .

I wrote, voiced, and produced this explainer as a part of Scripps' Disaster Prone project, which explored the implications of a steady increase in federal disaster declarations over the past five decades.

I designed and built this interactive visualization as a part of Scripps' Disaster Prone project, which explored the implications of a steady increase in federal disaster declarations over the past five decades. I collected, cleaned and analyzed data from the Federal Emergency Management Agency, and built the visualization using Highcharts and jQuery.

I pitched, wrote and produced this episode alongside DecodeDC host and founder Andrea Seabrook. We explore the origins of the popular "pull yourself up by your bootstraps" narrative in American politics, and what it means in an era of decreased economic mobility.

I created this graphic to accompany my reporting on the fast food worker prostests during the summer of 2014. Employees demanded what they called a "living wage" of $15 per hour. I used data from the U.S. Department of Labor to show how the purchasing power of the earnings from an hour of minimum-wage work has changed over time.

I created this infographic to accompany my reporting on the influx of unaccompanied minors from Central America seeking refuge in the U.S. in the summer of 2014. The data comes from the TRAC at Syracuse University, and I created the graphic using Adobe Illustrator.

I worked with Missourian reporter Tim Maylander and video producer Uno Yi to create this quick explanation of the federal government shutdown in the fall of 2013. Tim and I did the background reporting, I storyboarded and illustrated, and Uno shot and edited.
